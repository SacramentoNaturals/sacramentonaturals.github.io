Why Oil Cleansing Works for Every Skin Type

Published on November 18, 2025

The idea of using oil to cleanse your face may sound strange, especially if you struggle with oily skin. But oil cleansing works on a simple scientific principle: like dissolves like. This method effectively removes makeup, sunscreen, and excess sebum without stripping your skin.

The Science Behind Oil Cleansing

Oil binds to oil. When you massage an oil cleanser into your skin, it attaches to makeup, sunscreen, and buildup. Once emulsified with water, it rinses away cleanly, leaving your skin refreshed—not greasy.

Benefits for Every Skin Type

Dry Skin: Provides nourishment while cleansing, preventing tightness.

Oily Skin: Helps regulate oil production by avoiding harsh, stripping cleansers.

Sensitive Skin: Oil cleansers are typically free of aggressive surfactants, reducing irritation.

How to Use an Oil Cleanser

Apply the cleanser to dry skin and massage gently for 30–60 seconds. Add a bit of warm water to emulsify the oil, then rinse thoroughly. Follow with a water-based cleanser for a complete double cleanse.
